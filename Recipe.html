Brownies

Brownies are rich, chocolatey squares with a soft, fudgy center and a slightly firm top. They are made by melting dark chocolate and combining it with butter, sugar, eggs, and a small amount of flour and cocoa powder for structure. This balance of high-fat ingredients and low flour gives brownies their signature dense, gooey texture, as described in the recipe you are viewing

Preparation time: 10mins
Cooking Time: 20mins

Total Cooking Time: 30 mins

Servings 24 Brownies
Cost: Cheap

    Ingredients

  • 200g dark chocolate, we like at least 85%
  • 100g unsalted butter, very soft
  • 250g caster sugar
  • 4 large free range eggs, beaten to mix
  • 1tsp vanilla essence
  • 60g plain flour
  • 60g cocoa powder

    Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 180C (160C fan, Gas 4). Break up the dark chocolate into chunks and put into a heatproof bowl. Put the bowl on top of a saucepan filled with a quarter or less of water. Make sure the water doesn't touch the base of the bowl. Turn the heat up on the hob at a low temperature and simmer the water. This will melt the chocolate in the bowl. Continue to stir until all the chocolate has melted. Do not have the heat up too high otherwise, it will burn the chocolate. Alternatively, you can melt chocolate in the microwave.
  2. Leave to cool until needed but don't let the chocolate set as you need it to be melted to add to the brownie mix.
  3. Put the butter and sugar into the bowl of a food mixer and beat until fluffy.
  4. Gradually beat in the eggs, one at a time and ensure you beat well after each addition. Beat in the vanilla essence.
  5. Spoon the cooled melted chocolate into the mixture then mix it thoroughly. Sift in the flour and cocoa powder and gently fold together.
  6. When completely combined, spoon the mixture into a greased and lined 15cm square tin and spread the mixture evenly with the back of a spoon or spatula.
  7. Bake in the oven for about 20 minutes until the chocolate brownies are firm to the touch but still a bit fudgy.
  8. Remove the tin from the oven and set it on a wire cooling rack. Leave the chocolate brownies to cool completely before cutting them into pieces. Store the brownies in an airtight container and eat within 4 days.
